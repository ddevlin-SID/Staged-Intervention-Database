What is Staged Intervention?

Staged Intervention is the framework used in Scottish schools to identify and support pupils with additional support needs. Support is organised into four stages, with each stage bringing increased levels of intervention and involvement from specialists.

The Four Stages

Stage 4

Multi-Agency

Stage 3

Education Specialists

Stage 2

Additional Support

Stage 1

Universal Support

Most pupils are supported at Stage 1, with progressively fewer requiring higher stages of intervention.

1

Universal Support

Led by: Class Teacher

The class teacher meets the pupil's needs through quality first teaching and reasonable adjustments within the classroom.

Examples of Support:

Differentiated materials and tasks
Flexible seating arrangements
Visual timetables and schedules
Clear routines and expectations
Chunked instructions
Movement breaks
Check-ins during lessons
Peer support strategies
2

Additional Support

Led by: Class Teacher with Learning Support

The pupil receives additional support beyond universal classroom strategies, often involving the Learning Support department or coordinated interventions.

Examples of Support:

Targeted literacy or numeracy interventions
Small group support sessions
1:1 or withdrawal sessions (school staff)
Adapted resources and assessments
Regular monitoring and review
Enhanced home-school communication
Check-in/check-out systems
Referral to Pupil Support/Guidance
3

Targeted Support

Led by: Specialist School Staff & Education Professionals

Support involves school specialists or local authority education services, with more intensive or individualised approaches.

Examples of Support:

Educational Psychologist assessment
ASL Service involvement
Specialist sessions (e.g., dyslexia specialist)
Pupil Support Assistant allocation
Behaviour support planning
Wellbeing Assessment and Support Plan
Child's Planning Meeting
Coordinated Support Plan (CSP) consideration
4

Intensive Multi-Agency Support

Led by: External Agencies alongside School

Agencies from outside education are involved in supporting the pupil and family.

Examples of Support:

CAMHS (Child & Adolescent Mental Health)
Social Work
Speech & Language Therapy
Occupational Therapy
NHS Paediatrics
Voluntary sector organisations
Multi-agency planning meetings
Children's Hearing/Panel involvement
